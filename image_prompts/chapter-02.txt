A breathtakingly serene and divine cinematic scene depicting the multi-faced (Panchavaktra) Lord Hanuman as described in the Sri Parasara Samhita. In the center, Hanuman stands majestically with a brilliant golden aura, featuring five distinct faces (Monkey, Lion, Eagle, Boar, and Horse), each radiating wisdom and power. He has ten powerful arms holding sacred symbols like a sword, a shield, a Vedic book, and a golden pot of nectar (Amrita). He is adorned in rich yellow silks (Peetambara) and shimmering gem-studded crowns. Surrounding him, golden Sanskrit letters of his root mantras float in the air like glowing embers. The setting is a mystical, sun-drenched temple courtyard filled with blooming jasmine and soft incense smoke. In the foreground, a humble seeker is seated in a peaceful meditative posture, experiencing profound inner joy and "Paramananda." Art style: highly detailed, soulful, traditional Indian aesthetic, with vibrant, holy lighting and infinite tranquility.
